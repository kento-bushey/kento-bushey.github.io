San Diego City Streets Policy

Wheel Curbing Requirement

When parking on a hill or sloped street, San Diego law requires you to turn your wheels toward the curb (when facing downhill) or away from the curb (when facing uphill). This applies to all vehicles. Failure to curb your wheels can result in a citation even if you are otherwise legally parked. The current fine for a parking citation in San Diego is $90.

20-Foot Crosswalk Clearance

Do not park within 20 feet of a crosswalk, whether marked or unmarked. An unmarked crosswalk exists at any intersection where two streets meet, even if there are no painted lines.

72-Hour Parking Limit

No vehicle may remain parked on a San Diego city street for more than 72 consecutive hours (3 days).

In practice, the 72-hour rule is rarely enforced by parking enforcement. That said, it is still technically on the books — use your own judgment.

Source: San Diego Parking Enforcement

UCSD Campus Tips

Disability Placard Parking

A valid California disability placard grants free parking on campus in any A, B, S, Sr, Visitor, or Disabled space — no UCSD permit required. This applies throughout the campus, not just in designated disabled spots.

If approached, you are not required to answer any questions about your disability or placard. Simply display it visibly on your rearview mirror or dashboard.

Overnight Parking is Free

Parking enforcement on campus does not begin until 7 a.m. You can park on campus overnight for free and leave before enforcement starts. Hours by location:

• Main campus — weekdays 7 a.m. to 9 p.m., weekends 10:30 a.m. to 9 p.m.
• Scripps Institution of Oceanography — 7 a.m. to 9 p.m., 7 days a week
• UC San Diego Health (Hillcrest & La Jolla) — 24 hours, 7 days a week

No parking fee is required on university holidays. Source: UCSD Transportation
