San Diego City Streets Policy

Wheel Curbing Requirement

When parking on a hill or sloped street, San Diego law requires you to turn your wheels toward the curb (when facing downhill) or away from the curb (when facing uphill). This applies to all vehicles. Failure to curb your wheels can result in a citation even if you are otherwise legally parked. The current fine for a parking citation in San Diego is $90.

72-Hour Parking Limit

No vehicle may remain parked on a San Diego city street for more than 72 consecutive hours (3 days).

⚠️ In practice, the 72-hour rule is rarely enforced by parking enforcement. That said, it is still technically on the books — use your own judgment.
